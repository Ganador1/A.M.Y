1. Introduction
---------------

### Background

The use of nanoparticles as biomaterials for neural tissue regeneration has gained significant attention in recent years due to their ability to deliver growth factors to specific targets, promote cell survival and proliferation, and reduce inflammation. The aim of this study is to assess the efficacy of a biomaterial composed of functionalized nanoparticles for the regeneration of neural tissues, focusing on the promotion of neuronal growth and tissue repair.

### Research Questions

1. Will the use of functionalized nanoparticles in a biomaterial improve neurite outgrowth length and survival rate of neurons in the regenerated neural tissues?
2. What is the optimal concentration of functionalized nanoparticles for promoting neuronal growth and survival in the regenerated neural tissues?
3. Will the use of a specific type of growth factor for functionalization improve the efficacy of the biomaterial in promoting neuronal growth and survival?
4. What is the optimal duration of exposure to the biomaterial for achieving maximum effectiveness in promoting neuronal growth and survival?

### Hypotheses

1. The use of functionalized nanoparticles in a biomaterial will improve neurite outgrowth length and survival rate of neurons in the regenerated neural tissues compared to control groups.
2. The optimal concentration of functionalized nanoparticles for promoting neuronal growth and survival is higher than 0.1 ng/mL.
3. The use of a specific type of growth factor for functionalization will improve the efficacy of the biomaterial in promoting neuronal growth and survival compared to other types of growth factors.
4. The optimal duration of exposure to the biomaterial is 2 weeks for achieving maximum effectiveness in promoting neuronal growth and survival.

### Materials and Methods

1. Functionalized nanoparticles: We will use a biomaterial composed of functionalized nanoparticles, where each nanoparticle is coated with a specific type of growth factor for delivery to the targeted neural tissue. The concentration of functionalized nanoparticles will be varied from 0.1 ng/mL to 20 ng/mL
